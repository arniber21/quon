This depth-one QAOA circuit prepares a uniform superposition, applies a cost
layer over every pair in the triangle, and mixes with `Rx` rotations. The
[`pairs(n)` loop and sequential circuit composition](/language/#circuits-and-composition)
keep the graph-sized circuit explicit in its type.

## Source

The embedded program is
[`test/verify/qaoa.qn`](https://github.com/arniber21/quon/blob/main/test/verify/qaoa.qn),
an executable specialization of
[`frontend/tests/fixtures/qaoa.qn`](https://github.com/arniber21/quon/blob/main/frontend/tests/fixtures/qaoa.qn).

## Compile and simulate

```sh
./target/release/quonc test/verify/qaoa.qn --emit-qasm > /tmp/qaoa.qasm
QUONC=target/release/quonc python test/verify/qaoa.py
```

## Expected result

K3 has six optimal “one versus two” cuts: the bitstrings with Hamming weight
one or two. `000` and `111` cut no edges. With the fixture's selected angles,
each optimal bitstring should be more frequent than either non-optimal one.
The seeded
[`qaoa.py`](https://github.com/arniber21/quon/blob/main/test/verify/qaoa.py)
verifier compares those observed frequencies directly.
